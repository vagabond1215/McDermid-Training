Chapter 1 – Welcome

Welcome and congratulations! You are embarking on a career where your results mirror your effort. We expect you to work as diligently as we do. Training covers three key areas: Production, Resources, and Quality. Listen to your trainers, ask questions, stay positive, and follow the schedule to ensure success.

Training Expectations

  1. Learn the complete presentation—starting with Section A—by the end of your first week so you can present while in training and begin earning income.
  2. Collect at least five referrals per household.
  3. Book at least one referral during every virtual meeting.
  4. Complete training on time and be prepared for your release meeting.

Tasks

  • Complete the 1a Financial Analysis. Begin now so you can record recurring expenses you might overlook, such as haircuts. Exact amounts are not required; we will use your approximate monthly income requirement to build a reverse budget and determine the activity needed to reach your goals.
  • Add an alarm for the Professional Growth and Personal Development meeting. This meeting occurs every Friday at 11:00 a.m. Pacific. Set a reminder for 10 minutes beforehand and add it to your work calendar as a weekly recurring event.

Expectations

  • I understand that I must memorize and recite the script to qualify for field training.
  • I understand that I must integrate required forms into the presentation seamlessly using available technology.
  • I understand that I must set appointments that result in actual meetings.
  • I understand that field training is scheduled alongside my manager’s availability. If I cannot work my manager’s schedule, training may be delayed and I may not meet with clients or produce business.
  • I understand that without qualifying for field training I cannot meet with clients and a manager will not produce business for or with me.
  • I understand that completing field training allows me to work my own schedule, meet with my own clients, and produce business independently.
